Sentences

eg: By the early twentieth century, the question about the rate of evolution had been answered in favor of gradualism to most biologists’ satisfaction.

eg: Such changes are all but invisible over the short time scale of modern observations, and, it is argued, they are usually obscured by innumerable gaps in the imperfect fossil record.

普遍但是不可见的；在现代观察的短时间规模内；掩盖；

eg: Gradualism, with its stress on the slow pace of change, is a comforting position(立场), repeated over and over again in generations of(几代人的)textbooks.

eg: Sometimes a closed question must be reopened as new evidence or new arguments based on old evidence come to light(显露).

eg: In 1972 paleontologist(古生物学家) Stephen Jay Gould and Niles Eldredge challenged conventional wisdom(智慧，学问) with an opposing viewpoint, the punctuated(间断的) equilibrium hypothesis, which posits(断言，放置assert, affirm, declare) that species give rise to(导致) new species in relatively sudden bursts, without a lengthy transition period.

eg: These episodes of rapid evolution are separated by relatively long static spans(time intervals) during which a species may hardly change at all.

快速进化的时期被相当长的静态周期分隔开，在这段时期物种几乎不改变。

eg: Intermediated (中间的)fossil forms(形态，方式，格式), predicted by gradualism, are typically(往往，常常，典型的) lacking.

eg: If evolution had been a continuous, gradual process, one might expect that almost every fossil specimen would be slightly different from every year.

eg: If it seems difficult to conceive(想象) how(怎样) major changes could occur rapidly, consider this: an alteration of a single gene in files(in records) is enough to turn a normal fly with a single pair of wings into one that has two pairs of wings.

(喜欢，这个句型，同义词代换的好，句型又有变化)

Topic sentence

recent studies of evolution (punctuated or gradual)

Structure

the concept of the gradualism—punctuated equilibrium hypothesis—P example(clam and coral)— new evidence based on the old(horses)—understand above example—question—gradualism example—the connection of hypothesis

首先介绍进化渐进论的概念；

再提出与它相对的概念-间断平衡说；

再给出具体的例子来说明和用以前的例子来说明；

然后提出这两者之间的联系的疑问，到底是哪个；

然后提到渐进主义研究的例子；

最后做出假设以及要继续进行上述exhaustive研究；

文章结构：分总。

有种先说区别，最后一句总说归类

Exercises

HIGHLIGHTED SENTENCE

In 1972 paleontologist Stephen Jay Gould and Niles Eldredge challenged conventional wisdom with an opposing viewpoint, the punctuated equilibrium hypothesis, which posits that species give rise to new species in relatively sudden bursts, without a lengthy transition period.

A The punctuated equilibrium hypothesis challenged gradualism, which holds that species evolve in relatively sudden bursts of brief duration.

在看原文的时候，脑海里自然的想法就把间断平衡假说和突然改变联系在一起了，然后就觉得A,B都对，然而，这里出现了个迷路的which，这题ETS考的是语法，我晕，根据就近原则，which 是gradualism的从句。

B The punctuate equilibrium hypothesis, in opposition to gradualism, holds that transitions from one species to another occur in comparatively sudden burst.

C In 1972 Stephen Jay Gould and Niles Eldredge challenged gradualism by positing that change from one species to another cannot occur without a lengthy transition period.

这题，我的bug在于忽略了负负得正，not without = with，发现自己经常根据先入为主的印象判断问题，不能客观公正的看待题目的句子，这个区别和联系还是要注意。

According to paragraph 3, the lack of intermediate fossils in the fossil record of some species

A has been extensively studied by paleontologist for over a century(他们只是很熟悉那些化石但是并没有研究，而且通常都被忽视)

B contradicts the idea that most species have remained unchanged for millions of years(我的错误选项就是这个，后面的most species没有问题，但是它不是contradict，而是支持化石可能没改变，这个目的题还是要看题目的啊，加上出现原文的内容，所以我又自然对它有亲切感，但它并不是个好答案wake up)

C challenges the view that evolutionary change is gradual(例证挑战的都是以前的观点，老观点，支持现在的观点)

D is most common in the fossil records of clam and coral species (虽说以clam和coral为例，但是并没有说它的化石就缺失了，然而恰恰的相反，它的化石保存的很好，所以成为支持间断平衡假说的例证)

Learning Tips

1 把自己对文章理解的脉络梳理出之后，有利于对其他文章内容的大体预测，有点麻烦，但是要坚持；

2 大概三分之一的词句仍然不能做到快速阅读和理解，多看对记忆，增加频率；

3 对做题技巧不太熟悉，回去看书吧，边看边用；

4 理解时代词注意替换成实体，不然看快了容易忘记内容到底要说什么，从句的指代词也是要替换成实体，不然加几个插入语或者长从句，容易混淆。
